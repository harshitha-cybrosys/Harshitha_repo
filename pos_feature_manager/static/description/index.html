Employee POS Restrictions Tab

Open any employee record and navigate to the Point Of Sale tab to configure access permissions. Each boolean toggle independently controls a specific POS feature for that employee.

POS Settings — Employee Rights Tiers

Enable "Log in with Employees" in POS Settings and assign employees to Advanced, Basic, or Minimal rights tiers. Employees in the Advanced tier bypass all restrictions automatically.

Cashier Selection in POS Session

Switch between cashiers during an active POS session. Restrictions apply instantly after login — no restart needed. Advanced rights employees like the manager see no restrictions.

Numpad Disabled for Restricted Employee

When "Allow Numpad" is disabled, all number buttons (0–9), the decimal point, Qty, %, Price, and +/- mode buttons are grayed out and unclickable for that cashier.

Payment Restriction Warning Toast

When "Allow Payments" is disabled, clicking the Payment button or attempting to validate an order shows a clear warning toast: "You are not allowed to process payments."

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do I configure restrictions for an employee?

Go to Employees → Employees → [Employee Name] → Point Of Sale tab. You will find 8 boolean toggles. Uncheck any permission to restrict that feature for the employee. Save the record and open a POS session to test.

Why is the manager (Mitchell Admin) not being restricted even though I unchecked the booleans?

This is by design. Employees assigned to Advanced rights in POS Settings automatically bypass all restrictions, regardless of what booleans are set on their profile. This ensures managers are never accidentally locked out. To apply restrictions to an employee, move them to Basic or Minimal rights in POS Settings.

If numpad is disabled, can the employee still enter their PIN to log in?

Yes. The numpad restriction only applies to the Product Screen after login. The PIN entry numpad on the cashier login screen is never blocked, so employees can always log in with their PIN regardless of their numpad permission.

What happens when a restricted employee tries to process a payment?

The Payment button remains visible and clickable. When the employee clicks it, a warning toast appears saying "You are not allowed to process payments." The same warning appears if they try to validate the order on the payment screen. This provides clear feedback without breaking the POS UI.

Do restrictions update when switching cashiers without closing the session?

Yes. The module polls for cashier changes every 600ms. When a different employee logs in, their specific restrictions are applied within one second automatically — no session restart or page reload is needed.

What does the +/- button restriction do exactly?

The +/- button is used to set negative quantities or values in the POS — primarily for processing refunds or returns. Disabling it prevents cashiers from entering negative amounts, ensuring all returns must go through a manager or a separate approval process.
